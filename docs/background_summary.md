## What is already known

- **McEnteggart, Naeem et al. 2015 (J Vasc Interv Radiol).** Online patient-education materials for IR (peripheral arterial disease, central venous catheter, varicocele, uterine artery embolization, vertebroplasty, TIPS, DVT) read at grade levels of 9.0 (NLM) up to 15.0 (Wikipedia), all above the recommended 6th-grade level. Established the six-formula readability methodology that this project extends.
- **Piersson et al. 2025 (Eur Heart J Imaging Methods Pract).** Cardiac MRI patient pages across five sites also fail the 6th-grade benchmark; readability varies by site, with BHF and RadiologyInfo at opposite ends of FKRE/CLI.
- **Behers et al. 2024 (Cureus).** AI chatbot (ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot) answers to cardiac-catheterization patient questions are scored for readability — but the design measures chatbot *generation*, not chatbot *rewriting* of existing pages, and does not measure clinical accuracy of the chatbot output against a reference.
- **PEMAT (AHRQ).** Standardized instrument for understandability and actionability; complementary to formula-based readability. Not used as primary outcome here.

## What's the gap

1. Pre-procedure cardiac CT (TAVR planning, coronary CTA, LAAO/Watchman) patient education readability has not been systematically measured.
2. The trade-off between AI-rewriting for lower reading level and *preserving medical accuracy and completeness* has not been measured well in any cardiac imaging context.

## What this study adds

- **Aim 1.** First systematic six-formula readability survey of pre-procedure cardiac CT patient pages across the major US/UK patient sites.
- **Aim 2.** Quantifies whether three current-generation chatbots (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5 Pro) lower the reading level of those pages with a single locked prompt and reproducible parameters.
- **Aim 3 (novel core).** Blinded subspecialist scoring of accuracy and completeness of the AI rewrites, plus correlation of reading-level drop with accuracy — i.e., the trade-off that determines whether AI rewriting is actually safe to deploy to patients.
