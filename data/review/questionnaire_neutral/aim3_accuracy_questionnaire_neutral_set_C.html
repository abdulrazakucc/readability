Cardiac CT Patient-Education Readability Study · Aim 3

Clinical Accuracy Scoring of Patient Education Passages

Each item shows two versions of the same patient-education passage (coronary CTA, TAVR, LAAO/Watchman). Your job is the clinical question: when the passage was made easier to read, did it keep the medicine correct and complete?

Score each passage against its own reference on three 1–5 scales. You are blinded to the source of each passage — that is by design.

Assigned review setThis set contains 25 passages. Please score every item before downloading your responses.

1 Your details

Your name is saved only on this device and written into the CSV you download. Nothing is uploaded anywhere.

2 How to score

  • Read the reference passage (left) and the passage to score (right) for each item.
  • Score the passage on all three dimensions below using whole numbers 1–5.
  • Judge medical content only — not tone, not style, not reading level. Style concerns go in the notes box, never in the numbers.
  • Score each passage against its own reference only; do not compare passages to one another.
  • The items are in a fixed randomized order — do not try to guess where a passage came from.
Higher is better

Accuracy

Are all medical statements in the scored passage correct?
  • 5 Every statement accurate; no clinical misstatements.
  • 4 One minor inaccuracy; no change to understanding.
  • 3 One moderate inaccuracy; could confuse, not harm.
  • 2 Multiple inaccuracies, or one that could drive a wrong decision.
  • 1 Major inaccuracies likely to cause harm or refusal of care.
Higher is better

Completeness

Does the scored passage keep the key prep, risk, and safety points?
  • 5 All key prep / risk / safety points present.
  • 4 All major points present; one minor point dropped.
  • 3 One major point (prep step or risk) dropped.
  • 2 Multiple major points dropped; materially less informative.
  • 1 Omits most prep / risk / safety information.
Higher is worse · reverse-coded

Added errors

Does the scored passage add claims not in the reference?
  • 1 Nothing added; only content from the reference.
  • 2 One trivial added claim (e.g. wellness platitude).
  • 3 One non-trivial added medical claim.
  • 4 Multiple added claims, or one clearly wrong invented claim.
  • 5 Substantial fabricated medical content (hallucination).
Please add a note whenever you score ≤ 3 on Accuracy or Completeness, or ≥ 3 on Added errors. One line naming the specific problem (e.g. “says contrast is iodine-free — wrong” or “dropped the 45-day anticoagulation instruction”). These notes become the qualitative backbone of the paper.
Blinding: the blind ID on each card does not encode the source. Please do not seek out the answer key or any automated scores until after you submit — they would anchor your judgment.
